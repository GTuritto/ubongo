# Ubongo v0.5 — Trust Protocol, Chief of Staff, and Identity (Plan v2, approved)

Approved 2026-06-12. Reconciled against the actual codebase: v0.1.5, ~15,400 LOC, 960 tests
green, 16 ADRs, LOC soft budget crossed, openspec ledger stale, v0.2 (Telegram) unstarted.
The original plan assumed greenfield. The real project is past its own v0.1 and most of the
brainstorm's trust protocol either already exists under different names, conflicts with an
accepted ADR, or converges with work the project's own architecture review already proposed.
This version maps the brainstorm onto reality, names the conflicts as decisions rather than
burying them, and re-sequences around the project's own constraints.

Core principle unchanged: **fixed floor, learned ceiling**. The codebase already embodies the
floor (ADRs 0005, 0006, 0013: sandbox in code, human-only promotion, human-only allowlist
changes). What is missing is the part that makes the floor livable: persistent grants.

---

## What the brainstorm maps to in the existing code

Already built, fully or mostly: tool synthesis is `authoring/` (quarantine, side-effect-free
scoring, human approval — the manifest-and-gate flow, shipped). Ephemeral workers are the agent
fleet under the six runner modes. The single external seam is the Connector (ADR-0016). Secrets
live in `.env` only. Self-modification with a human gate is the GP loop plus authoring, both
boot paused. The shell envelope is `sandbox.py`. Single-writer durable state and the universal
delivery queue (ADR-0002) are exactly the "everything legible through one seam" instinct,
already enforced in code.

Partially built: governance scores risk, confidence, and reversibility per turn, but there is
**no grant registry** — and this is the single most important finding of the reconciliation.
The current approval gate is ask-every-time, per turn, interactive. That is precisely the
blanket ask-and-wait model the brainstorm rejected for fatigue. Grants bound to capability plus
consequence class, persisting until revoked, checked at execution time, are the brainstorm's
genuine contribution to this codebase: they are what turns the existing gate from a ceremony
generator into a trust protocol. Conveniently, they are also what ADR-0016 explicitly deferred
(per-call pre-execution gating, tool-level allowlists) and what the architecture review's
candidate 4 names as the predictable bending point (approval is synchronous, channel-smeared,
and cannot resume).

Not built and uncontroversial: the outer network envelope (egress allowlist — SECURITY.md
itself flags that whatever the Connector's model plans is what leaves the machine),
plan-preview and completion narration, standing jobs, the verbosity knob, backup and migration
tooling, identity work.

Not built and in conflict with accepted ADRs, requiring an explicit decision before any code:

**Decision 1 — tiered installs versus the human-only allowlist.** The brainstorm wanted
apt-narrates-and-proceeds. ADR-0005/0006 say the sandbox allowlist is a human-only change,
which is stricter. Decision: **keep the ADR.** The codebase's posture is the right one for a
system whose identity is governed self-modification; the brainstorm's tiering lives one level
up, as Ubongo *proposing* allowlist additions through the same quarantine-and-approve flow
authoring already uses. Capability acquisition stays cold and gated; only the proposal is
autonomous.

**Decision 2 — Chief of Staff as a subsystem versus recognizing it already exists.** Master is
the Chief of Staff. The fleet is the staff. Authoring is how staff acquire tooling, and the
skill library is the estate that survives them. The genuine deltas are chartered persistence (a
role with scoped working memory and a defined recurring duty) and grant attenuation per agent
(a staffer holds a subset of grants, never more). Both are thin layers over the registry once
it exists, not a new subsystem. Decision: **build no Chief of Staff package.** The LOC budget
is crossed and the project's rule is cut, don't expand; charters become registry rows plus
routing config, and the framing lives in docs, not in `src/`.

**Decision 3 — the Podman envelope versus the no-Docker posture.** ADR-0001's exclusions are
about orchestration frameworks; the stated absence of Docker is about the dependency tree, not
about deployment. A Podman quadlet wrapping the existing process, with a UID-keyed nftables
egress allowlist, is infrastructure outside `src/`, zero LOC against the budget, and it closes
the one hole the project has already documented (Connector arguments leave the machine
controlled only by per-server flags). Decision: **do it**, and record in an ADR that it changes
deployment, not architecture — including the platform asymmetry named in Amendment 1 below.

## Phases, re-sequenced

The original ordering survives in spirit (floor before fun) but the project's own findings
reorder the work: the ledger fix is free, the store split is both overdue and the prerequisite
for identity work, and the approval seam is the declared first task of v0.2.

### Phase 00: Reconcile the ledger

An afternoon. Archive `openspec/changes/complete-fanout-peer-replacement/` (the work is
implemented and tested in all five fan-out modes; the proposal was never closed). Fix the
`runner.py` "Phase 13 may revisit" docstrings to state the accepted asymmetry, and the STATE.md
ADR count (sixteen, not twelve). Zero behavioral change, net-negative on the active set, and it
stops the repo misleading its own reviews — it already misled one.

Exit criteria: one version of the truth across ledger, docstrings, and STATE.md.

### Phase 01: The outer envelope

Days. Per Decision 3: dedicated `ubongo` user, Podman rootless quadlet around the existing
systemd setup, nftables egress allowlist keyed on the UID (OpenRouter, plus each MCP server the
Connector is configured for, and nothing else). Secrets stay in `.env` but the mount is
read-only into the container and the allowlist makes exfiltration paths enumerable. This is
deployment work, zero `src/` LOC. **Linux/Pi deployment only — see Amendment 1.**

The terrarium falls out here, earlier than in plan v1: the same quadlet with a looser
allowlist, inert keys, and the daemons unpaused is the growth experiment, available as soon as
this phase closes.

Exit criteria: on the Linux deployment, Ubongo cannot reach a non-allowlisted host, verified by
attempt; a Connector tool call to an unconfigured destination fails at the network layer, not
at the model's discretion. New ADR recorded, naming the macOS gap.

### Phase 02: Split the store

One to two weeks. The architecture review's candidate 1, trigger fired: `store.py` at 1,990
lines and 92 functions splits along subsystem seams (conversation/session/recall core, trace,
evolution state, authoring state, index metadata). Net LOC neutral, behavior-free, callers
re-import. **Candidate 3 (the shared judgment-parsing module under both evaluation sandboxes,
net −20 LOC) rides along — see Amendment 2.**

This earns its place in a trust-protocol plan for a reason beyond hygiene: it is the
state-layering work from the identity phase, arrived early. Backup, migration, forking, and
grants-don't-migrate all require that skills, memories, evolution state, and (future) grant
records be separable artifacts. The split is that separation. Doing it now means Phase 07 is
tar-and-restore instead of archaeology.

Exit criteria: each seam has its own module and test file, ADR-0002's single-writer rule
untouched, the monolithic test module split along the same lines.

### Phase 03: The approval seam

One week, and it is formally the first task of v0.2 — the review's candidate 4, unchanged in
shape. Promote the approval exchange from a dict on Response to a typed, persisted, resumable
seam in `governance/approval.py`: a frozen ApprovalRequest, a pending record keyed by
decision_id, one resume function. REPL and web become the two proving adapters. **The pending
record is the source of truth, not channel session state, and pending approvals get a listable
surface (`/pending` or equivalent) — see Amendment 5.**

This is the load-bearing prerequisite for everything the brainstorm wanted asynchronously:
approve-later is what lets a standing job park and raise, what lets Telegram carry a y/n/why,
and what unblocks the MCP dead end (gated=true, no path forward). Grants (Phase 05) hang their
first-encounter asks on this same seam.

Exit criteria: an approval requested in one channel and resolved in another, headlessly
testable, no channel re-implementing the resume contract.

### Phase 04: Telegram (v0.2 proper)

Two to three weeks. The spec's discipline wins: transport adapter over `channel.run_turn`,
`before_send` policy handler on the named events, `allowed_user_ids` restored. This is also
where the trust posture knowingly changes — first cloud-relayed channel, auth returns, and the
envelope from Phase 01 is what makes that acceptable.

For the trust protocol, Telegram is not just a feature: it is the channel through which
park-and-raise, completion narration, and asynchronous asks actually reach you. The
brainstorm's "blocked work becomes conversation" has no delivery mechanism until this exists.
The queue seam was built for it.

Exit criteria: a gated turn raised over Telegram and approved there via the Phase 03 seam,
quiet hours respected, auth enforced.

### Phase 05: The grant registry, and the Connector armed

Two to three weeks. The heart of the brainstorm lands here, and it lands as ADR-0016's
deferrals coming due rather than as a new idea. A grants table (in its own store module, per
Phase 02): capability class, consequence class, scope, date, purpose, status. Execution-time
checks in the Connector's dispatch path and the governance evaluation: first encounter of a
capability class asks through the Phase 03 seam; granted classes proceed with narration;
revocation propagates and survives restarts; read grants generalize liberally, write grants
narrowly. Tool-level allowlists per MCP server, which ADR-0016 parked until real tool names
exist — they will exist the moment the first integration (Calendar, Gmail, the news digest) is
configured, which is the natural trigger for this phase. **This phase gets its own ADR
(extending 0004 and 0016) and is paired with a named cut — see Amendments 3 and 4.**

Decision 2 is implemented here as configuration, not code: a chartered role is an agent entry
with a scoped grant subset and a recurring duty named in routing config. Attenuation is a
registry constraint (an agent's grants must be a subset of the global set). Hiring is proposing
such an entry through the approval seam; firing is revoking it, with authored skills remaining
in the library. The Chief of Staff ships as roughly zero new modules.

Exit criteria: no Connector call executes without a registry check; a revoked grant kills or
parks dependent work visibly; "what can you do and why" answerable in one turn; an agent
demonstrably cannot exercise a grant the global registry lacks.

### Phase 06: Standing jobs

One to two weeks. Proactive output through the existing queue, scheduled by the existing daemon
lifecycle (a fourth DaemonLoop, booting paused like its siblings — the house style).
Definition-time grant bundles: creating a standing job states its needs, approved once through
the seam; execution within the bundle proceeds with one-line completion narration; anything
outside parks the job and raises it over Telegram. The news digest is the first candidate and
the acceptance test.

Exit criteria: a daily job runs unattended inside its bundle, parks gracefully on a missing
grant, raises itself in conversation, and reports completions in one line.

### Phase 07: The contract and identity

Ongoing, and deliberately last. The verbosity knob per domain, manual first, as governance
config. Learned legibility later and only for verbosity, with patterns named explicitly before
adoption and learned rules landing as legible registry entries — the GP loop is the natural
machinery, and the fixed floor is already enforced by ADRs 0006 and 0013, so the dangerous half
of "learning the boundaries" is structurally impossible in this codebase.

Identity and portability ride on Phase 02's layering: backup is the SQLite file, the vault,
config, and the skill library; migration replays config rather than an install log (Decision 1
means there is no autonomous install log, only the human-approved allowlist, which is itself
config). Grants do not migrate; a moved instance re-asks per capability class on the new
envelope. Forking, naming as a speech act, and inter-instance skill exchange remain
designed-but-deferred until a second instance has a reason to exist; when it does, authored
skills already carry quarantine-and-approve as their import path.

## Amendments resolved at approval (2026-06-12)

1. **Platform asymmetry named.** Podman quadlets and nftables exist on the Linux/Pi deployment
   only; Ubongo also runs on macOS, where the Phase 01 envelope does not apply. The Decision 3
   ADR must state this explicitly: the egress guarantee holds on one host class. No macOS
   equivalent (pf anchors) is planned.
2. **Budget honesty gets teeth.** The plan's one defended expenditure is the grant registry.
   In exchange: candidate 3 (shared judgment parsing, net −20 LOC) ships with Phase 02, and
   Phase 05 is paired with a named cut — **delete the `retry:repair` evolvable target** and its
   structural-proxy fitness machinery (acknowledged in ADR-0007 as the weakest signal in the
   system). The deletion gets a line in the Phase 05 ADR.
3. **Versioning policy.** Phases 00–02 ship as v0.1.x increments (v0.1.6, v0.1.7). Phases
   03–04 ship as **v0.2** (Telegram). Phase 05 ships as **v0.3** (the grant registry; this
   re-stamps the old roadmap's "v0.3 = proactive output" label). Phase 06 ships as **v0.4**
   (standing jobs). Phase 07 completes as **v0.5**, the plan's namesake. STATE.md notes the
   re-stamping when Phase 05 lands.
4. **ADRs required.** Phase 01: the deployment envelope (Decision 3 + Amendment 1). Phase 05:
   grants move governance from gate-at-delivery to check-before-execution for Connector calls,
   extending ADRs 0004 and 0016; recorded as its own ADR, including the retry:repair cut.
5. **Phase 03 hidden requirement made explicit.** Cross-channel resume implies pending
   approvals are listable (`/pending` or equivalent) and that the persisted pending record —
   not channel session state — is the source of truth, because the requesting channel may be
   gone (one-shot already exited).
6. **Branch and phase naming.** v0.1's `phase-N-<name>` namespace (0–21) is taken. This plan
   uses two-digit phases on `v0.5/NN-<name>` branches (e.g. `v0.5/00-reconcile-ledger`), draft
   PR opened after the first commit per the house workflow, user merges.

## Sequencing rules and budget honesty

Phase 00 is free and immediate. Phase 01 is zero-LOC infrastructure. Phase 02 is net-neutral
(net-negative with candidate 3) and overdue by the project's own trigger. Phases 03 through 06
are v0.2 through v0.4 as re-stamped, with the registry as the one genuinely new subsystem — it
should be small, it lives in the store seams Phase 02 creates, and it is the budget expenditure
this plan defends, paid for by the retry:repair cut: it converts an ask-every-time gate into
persistent consent, which is the difference between a governed system you operate and a
companion you live with. Everything else the brainstorm produced is either already in the tree,
a configuration of it, or documentation.

Rough total for Phases 00 through 06: eight to ten weeks at evenings-and-weekends pace, of
which roughly half was already on the project's own roadmap before the brainstorm existed. That
overlap is the strongest evidence the protocol fits the architecture: the codebase and the
conversation kept arriving at the same seams independently.
